Key Responsibilities:

  • Communicate with stakeholders through comments
  • Ensure deliverables meet quality standards
  • Maintain accurate due dates and assignments
  • Participate in monthly progress reviews

This guide provides a simplified 5-step process to get started in Asana and maintain your 4-Year Strategic Plan tasks effectively.

Step 1: Access Your Strategic Plan Tasks

Getting Started:

  • Log into Asana using your @tn.gov email address
  • Navigate to your assigned project (corresponds to a Strategic Plan Goal)
  • Locate your action steps listed as subtasks under relevant objectives

What You'll See:

  • Each action step is listed as a subtask, identified by a unique Asana Task ID that serves as its primary key‚Äîensuring every step in the 24-27 TDH Strategic Plan can be distinctly referenced and efficiently managed.
  • Each subtask shows assignee (you), due date, and current status
  • Custom fields track TDH identifiers and CFG metrics

üí° Tip: Favorite or Bookmark your main strategic plan projects for easy access

Step 2: Create and Update Tasks

Essential Task Information:

  • Clear, descriptive title
  • Due date (realistic and achievable)
  • Description section describing the deliverable
  • Assignee (you as the project lead)
  • Collaborators (key stakeholders who need updates)

Task Creation Steps:

  1. Click '+ Add subtask' within the appropriate objective
  2. Fill in title, description, and due date
  3. Assign to yourself as project lead
  4. Add relevant custom fields (TDH ID, Status, Quarter)

TDH Status: What to Pick and When

Use TDH Status to reflect current state of the subtask. Update whenever you start work, hit a delay, or finish. This field feeds leadership dashboards.

Not Started

Assigned but no work has begun.

When to use
  • Task is acknowledged and has a due date, but work has not begun.
  • Waiting on kickoff from your team or initial info.
In Progress

Work has started and is moving.

When to use
  • You or your team have begun work on the deliverable.
  • No known blockers preventing progress.
On Track

On schedule with clear path to finish.

When to use
  • Planned finish date is achievable with current resources.
  • Dependencies are known and lined up.
Off Track

Behind schedule; plan needs correction.

When to use
  • Due date is unlikely to be met without changes.
  • Scope or staffing changed and timeline slipped.
At Risk

Could miss target if issues persist.

When to use
  • Upcoming dependency might slip or information is pending.
  • New risks identified; mitigation underway.
Blocked

Cannot proceed until something unblocks.

When to use
  • Waiting on external approval, deliverable, or access.
  • Technical or policy barrier prevents work from continuing.
Completed

Deliverable finished and accepted.

When to use
  • Work is done, reviewed as needed, and no further action remains.
  • Attach final file or link before setting to Completed.

Step 3: Use Comments for Communication

Why Comments Matter:

  • Replaces email chains and meeting back-and-forth
  • Keeps all project communication in one place
  • Automatically notifies task collaborators of updates
  • Maintains continuous record of decisions and progress

Best Practices:

  • @mention specific people for targeted responses
  • Ask questions directly instead of scheduling meetings
  • Share progress updates weekly or bi-weekly
  • Document key decisions and reasoning
  • Tag urgent items that need immediate attention

Example Comments:

Progress Update:

"Week 3 update: Completed stakeholder interviews (5/7). Remaining interviews scheduled for next Tuesday. Draft analysis will be ready by Friday. @John.Doe - can you review the preliminary findings?"

Help Request:

"@Team - I'm blocked on getting approval from Legal. Has anyone worked with them on similar policy reviews? Need guidance on next steps."
